# JTBD primer (for product root work)

Short orientation for assistants drafting or reviewing **[`../assets/product-root-jtbd.md`](../assets/product-root-jtbd.md)**. This follows the **Strategyn / Ulwick** style of **Jobs-to-be-Done** and **Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI)**—other JTBD schools differ.

## Template shape (one outline, optional depth)

The JTBD product root is a **single** template: **Job executor** (core job with optional **job step** groups and outcome rows) → optional **related** and **emotional/social** jobs under the same executor → **Product lifecycle support team** → **Buyer** → **Boundaries** → **Outcomes**. **Omit** any block that does not help this repo—narrow scopes may keep only **Core functional job** + **Outcomes**; complex markets may use every section.

## Why jobs

- **Unit of analysis** is the **core functional job** people want done—not the product name or feature list.
- A **market** is **people** + **that job** (not only a product category).
- The job is **solution-agnostic** and comparatively **stable**; solutions change.

## Job statement

Use a clear grammatical pattern:

**verb + object + optional contextual clarifier**

Keep **altitude** honest: **too narrow** (what our product happens to do today) hides substitutes; **too broad** is impossible to serve. Do not define the job only by asking what someone hired *your* product to do.

## Roles (three customer types in ODI)

| Role | In this framework |
|------|-------------------|
| **Job executor** | Performs the **core functional job** with a solution. |
| **Product lifecycle support** | Installs, moves, maintains, upgrades, disposes of—**consumption chain** around the offering. |
| **Buyer** | **Purchase decision**—often financial criteria. **Not** the same as the core job; understanding buying does not replace understanding the job. |

The template maps **executor** (including core job + related + emotional under **Job Executor**) and splits **lifecycle** and **buyer** into their own **`##`** sections when separate roles matter.

## Job map vs journey

Under **Core functional job**, **job step** groups (`####`) are **process**—what must happen for the job to be done. They are **not** a marketing journey, onboarding flow, or purchase funnel. A common eight-step scaffold is **Define → Locate → Prepare → Confirm → Execute → Monitor → Modify → Conclude**—use, rename, or skip steps; group them into **`####`** blocks for readability.

## Needs = desired outcomes

**Needs** for planning are **desired outcome statements**: success metrics for getting the job done, **without naming your product** as the need. Outcome rows may sit **under job step groups** and/or in the final **Outcomes** index—**one** coherent set for the product tree.
